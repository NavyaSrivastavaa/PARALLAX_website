Parallax Scrolling Effect

Parallax scrolling is a technique used in web design to create an engaging and dynamic user experience. It involves the background of a webpage moving at a different speed than the foreground content as the user scrolls down the page. This creates an illusion of depth and adds a sense of interactivity and visual interest to the website. The term "parallax" refers to the apparent displacement or difference in the position of an object when viewed from different angles. In the context of web design, it means that as users scroll down a parallax website, different elements on the page move at varying speeds, giving the impression of a multi-layered, 3D-like environment.
